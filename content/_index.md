## Qualifying Procedure

The Daytona 500‚Äôs qualifying system is unique in NASCAR, incorporating both single-car speed runs and short qualifying races to set the starting grid:

1.  **Single-Car Qualifying**
    *   Every team goes out alone to post their fastest lap.
    *   The fastest driver earns the pole position.
    *   The second-fastest driver earns the outside front row.
    These two positions are locked in heading into the Duel races.

2.  **Duel at Daytona**
    Also known as the Duel Races, this pair of 150-mile races sets the remainder of the field:
    *   The finish order of Duel Race 1 determines inside row starters.
    *   Duel Race 2 finish order fills the outside row starters.
    Positions not locked in by qualifying times are assigned based on finishing positions in these Duels.

This mixed system blends pure speed with strategic racing, as drafting and track position become essential.
